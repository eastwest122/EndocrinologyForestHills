Frequently Asked Questions

An endocrinologist is a specialist who diagnoses, treats, and manages conditions related to the body’s endocrine (hormone) system. This includes disorders of the thyroid (hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, thyroid nodules), pancreas (diabetes management), adrenal glands (Cushing’s, Addison’s), pituitary gland issues, metabolic disorders (like obesity and lipid imbalances), osteoporosis, hormone imbalances, and reproductive hormone disorders.
You should consider seeing an endocrinologist if: your primary care provider suspects a hormone-related issue (high or low hormone levels) you have difficulty managing diabetes or your blood sugar remains uncontrolled despite treatment you have thyroid symptoms (fatigue, weight changes, temperature sensitivity, nodules) symptoms of adrenal or pituitary gland problems show up (abnormal growth, mood shifts, unexplained weight gain or loss) you notice bone weakening or risk of osteoporosis you have unexplained weight changes, especially in metabolic disorders or hormone imbalance contexts.
During your initial visit with an endocrinologist, you can typically expect: a review of your medical history, symptoms, medications, and family history a physical examination laboratory tests (blood, urine) to measure hormone levels possibly imaging tests (ultrasound, MRI, CT) if needed, especially for thyroid, adrenal, or pituitary concerns a discussion of diagnosis, treatment options, lifestyle adjustments, and a plan moving forward.
